Trailblazers of the Mount

Pat Shibinski

Pat Shibinski was a foundational figure in women’s athletics at MSJ. She helped establish early programs and led the physical education department, shaping opportunities for generations of athletes.

Jean Dowell

Jean Dowell played a critical role in developing women’s athletics both at MSJ and beyond. She advocated for opportunities for women in sports and helped expand programs during a time when opportunities were limited.

Kay Corcoran

Kay Corcoran was a key figure in the success of early women’s athletics at the Mount, contributing to competitive teams and helping establish the program’s reputation.

Interview Insights

Interviews with Jean Dowell and John Spinney highlighted the importance of early leadership and persistence in building women’s athletics.

Dowell described how early programs operated with limited resources but strong dedication, emphasizing that “there was never a lack of interest, but a lack of opportunity.”

She also explained that Title IX “changed everything,” providing recognition and structure that women’s athletics previously lacked.

Both interviews emphasized that the success of today’s programs is rooted in the efforts of early athletes and coaches who built opportunities from the ground up.

Legacy Today

The legacy of early women’s athletics at MSJ continues today. The success of modern teams reflects the foundation built by trailblazers who pushed for opportunity, recognition, and equality.

As Dowell suggested, success builds on itself, and the achievements of early athletes continue to inspire current and future student-athletes at the Mount.
